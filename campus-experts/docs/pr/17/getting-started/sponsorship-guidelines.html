# Requesting monetary support for your event

# Disclaimer

GitHub is happy to provide funding for Campus Expert initiatives as providing funding to you:

  • Makes it easier for you to organise events, knowing that support is there.
  • Gives you more time to focus on the event, and less on raising sponsorship.
  • Having GitHub sponsor your event makes it easier to attract future sponsors, as it adds legitimacy.

However, funding from GitHub is not intended to be the sole funding for your events. This is due to the follow reasons:

  • The money isn't unlimited. We need to work together to use the Campus Expert budget well, so that it goes as far as possible, and helps as many students as possible.
  • Dependence on GitHub. If you rely solely on us for funding, and don't develop relationships with other sponsors, you are eventually going to run into problems when you need more than we can provide.
  • Reduced opportunities. New sponsors bring new technology, new jobs, and new opportunities into your communities. By limiting yourselves to GitHub, your community loses out on diverse opportunities.
  • Careful spending. It's easy to get complacent when money is too easily available. Costs start to creep up and quotes are less competitive. This will harm future Experts as less budget will be available.

# Guidelines

As mentioned above, the budget for the program is not unlimited. To make sure that the maximum number of communities can be supported, we have the following general guidelines:

  • Max $1000 per Campus Expert in total per two months.
  • You must write a Post-Event Report. If you don't write post-event reports, you will not be eligible for requesting sponsorship or swag.

Note: Keepign your expenses under $1000 does not mean that your request is automatically approved.

Expenses must be approved in advance. You will likely be required to provide an itemized invoice of your expenses. Reimbursement is not an excuse to book a 5 star hotel, or a premium restaurant. We want you to be safe, comfortable, and happy, but we also want to help as many students as possible. Please help us help you by spending responsibly 💖
Whilst ensuring we spend responsibly is important, getting the cheapest price is not the #1 thing. When booking travel especially, follow these priorities:

  1. SAFETY: Your safety is more important than saving money. Never book a more dangerous option to get a cheaper price.
  2. COMFORT: We want you to be healthy and happy. If a direct flight is a little more than a transfer that will add hours to your journey, get the direct flight.
  3. TIME: You're all incredibly busy already. If saving money means adding lots of time to your journey, take the shorter route.
  4. COST: Only after you've accounted for your safety, comfort, and time, should you worry about the cost.

Once the expense is approve, you can go ahead and make the purchase and GitHub will reimburse Campus Experts for costs related to the event. Please keep receipts for all your expenses, as you are required to provide them when requesting reimbursement via the Expenses Process

Some examples of situations where you will need to keep your receipts:

  • Travel for scholarships like GitHub Universe, or local travel to an event
  • Small costs of running an event, eg. ordering food, renting a university auditorium
  • Reusable materials for multiple events, eg. a rollup banner

Events that are higher in costs, and have sponsorship packages can be provided with sponsorship (i.e., you will receive the funds upfront).
