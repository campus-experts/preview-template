Expenses and Reimbursements

Requesting Reimbursement

All expenses must be approved before the event. Expenses not pre-approved will not be reimbursed. Refer to the sponsorship guidelines to see how to request a reimbursement

Onboarding to Payment Central

  • Head over to the campus-experts.github.ioopen in new window repo's issuesopen in new window page
  • Create a new issue, and select the ðŸ’°Onboarding Payment Central option.
  • Fill it out, and submit!
  • Check your email in a few days for an email from Microsoft Payment Central, and fill in all the required banking details.
  • If asked to fill the SSPA form ping @MetzinAround to check with the Microsoft team. Important: Double-check that the bank account and details on the form are correct. We will send all payments to the account added to this form, and changes will require you to create a new account on Payment Central. If you need to update the bank or personal information, contact @MetzinAround to start an update request.

Tracking your Expenses

You will need access to Campus Experts Receipts folder on Google Drive, which can be found hereopen in new window If you do not have access to this folder, ping @MetzinAround on the GitHub Education Discord.

  • Ensure that you have opened an issue, and have been approved for monetary support
  • Save your receipts!
  • Create an invoice using this templateopen in new window
  • Create a new folder in the Receipts folderopen in new window with the title: YOUR NAME - EVENT NAME - DATE OF EVENT
  • Upload your receipts to the newly created folder
  • Fill out the Expense Reporting Formopen in new window

Getting Paid - Campus Experts from the US

  • Create an invoice for the total amount, this invoice should include a list of all expenses and their amounts.
  • Fill out the invoice with the following details, as shown in the screenshot:
    Attn: Pj Metz
    GitHub Inc,
    88 Colin P Kelly Jr Street,
    San Francisco,
    CA 94107,
    NORTH AMERICA
    (This does not mean that you need to physically mail your invoice in, but digital invoices must also be addressed here)
  • For the Invoice number, this indicates how many invoices you have sent the program. So use the number that indicates how many invoices you have sent. Your first invoice should be "001". The next time you submit an invoice it should be "002" and so on and so forth. If you have already been sending invoices you do not need to start the numbering over. As long as each invoice has a different number, that is fine
  • The line items in the invoice do not need to say "Dates Worked." Rather, it should be formatted to say what the line item is for, and a total cost for that particular line item.
  • To get the total cost in the Invoice, work backwards from the total cost. If the pizza was $250, and the event was 5 hours, then the hourly rate should be $50. Inputting the hourly rate and the hours will automatically fill out the total at the end of the row.
  • Invoices are paid in 30 days, sometimes longer for international students.
  • Email invoices@github.com, cc MetzinAround@github.com with:
    • Your completed invoice.
    • Attach the receipts (note that this is in addition to uploading them to the Google Drive mentioned above).
  • Please set the subject line to: YOUR NAME - MM/DD/YY OF INVOICE - being-an-expert ISSUE NUMBER
