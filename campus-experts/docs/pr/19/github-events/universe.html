# GitHub Universe 🌃

GitHub Universe is the annual conference and event hosted by GitHub and a major event in the software development industry where GitHub showcases its latest features, updates, and announcements, as well as provides a platform for discussions and networking among developers, software engineers, project managers, and other professionals in the tech community.

Universe brings together the world’s developers, enterprise leaders, and security professionals for two days of interactive sessions on AI,security, and the developer experience.

  • Official Websiteopen in new window

# Opportunity 💡

Every year, we have the opportunity to bring a few Campus Experts to the event with all expenses included.

Joining for GitHub Universe serves as a great way to meet the people behind some of the tools and features that you use, great open source developers and other Campus Experts. It is also a great opportunity to share the stories from your community and the work you've done as a Campus Expert to build safe spaces, outstanding events and helping others to find their space in tech.

Expenses included

  • Transportation to the San Francisco (flights/train) 🛫
  • Hosting (Hotel/Hostel) 🏨
  • Ticket for the conference 🎟️
  • Local transportations (You'll receive an all included local transportation card) 🚃
  • Meals during the conference 🍔
  • Activities ✨

# Interest Form 📝

Between one and three months before the event, the GitHub Education team will post an issue on the being-an-expertopen in new window repository giving general details about the event and the will publish a form requesting Campus Experts interested in attending the conference to sign up. The form, serves as a way to collect general details about you and your travel requirements while also helping the team plan the logistics.

TIP

We try our best to accomodate as many Campus Experts as possible to attend the conference, yet, budget and event capacity is limited.

# Selected ✅

If you get selected to attend the conference, you will be mentioned on the issue and you'll have to confirm your attendance to the conference. You can do so by reaching out to the GitHub Education team. It is until this point that you can purchase your flight to the conference. We recommend you buy the tickets as soon as possible due to the volatility of flight prices.

WARNING

If the cost of attending the conference significantly increases (> $450 USD). Reach out to the GitHub Education team. To verify it is within budget.

We ask you to bring the next items:

  • GitHub Campus Experts Hoodie
  • GitHub Campus Expert sports shirt
  • Swag from your community or your events!

# Invitation Letters

Some countries might require you to get a Visa or electronic travel authorization (ETA) to enter the US. We are able to facilitate an invitation letter to support your travel Visa request process. Reach out to the GitHub Education team to request a letter. It normally takes between 3-5 days to get a letter.

# Travel to/from the airport 🛫

It's recommended that you use Lyft or Uber to get from the airport to the hostel. This will be expenseable via the expenses processopen in new window. Please download either app and set up a payment card in it before you fly.

There is free WiFi at SFO airport to request the vehicle.

# Travelling to and from Universe 🚎

When travelling to/from the conference and hotel, you will ride in a public transport with other Campus Experts, You can also walk your way to the event but its a long walk. Universe will take place at Yerba Buena Center for the Artsopen in new window: 701 Mission St, San Francisco, CA 94103. It's 30 min by public transport 😃

# From Hotel to GitHub HQ :octocat:

It's approximately a 30 min tram to the office. The GitHub Officeopen in new window is in an area known as SOMA. SOMA also has problem spots, but there is also not much to see there besides the GitHub Office. Address: 88 Colin P Kelly Jr St, San Francisco, CA 94107

# Tips for safety 🚨

San Francisco is generally a safe city. But like any city, it has more dangerous areas. When in San Francisco, follow these tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings. Do not walk alone on your phone.
  • Travel in groups.
  • Secure your belongings. Don't leave phones and wallets in back pockets.
  • Use Lyft and Uber rather than walking if you feel unsafe.
    • Remember you are in town on a GitHub trip: we want to look after you, and will pay for your transport.

# Risky areas 🛑

You might have heard of the Tenderloinopen in new window. The Tenderloin has a reputation as one of the riskier areas of San Francisco. The Tenderloin also encompasses tourist destinations such as Union Square and the Civic Center. Follow the above advice at all times: travel in groups, stay aware while you reach your destination. The main spot to avoid in this area is the intersection of Turk and Taylor Street.

# Hotel 🏨

The hotel is Courtyard by Marriott San Francisco Fisherman's Wharfopen in new window, booked between the 6th and 10th of November. The address is 580 Beach St, San Francisco, CA 94133.

Set in Fisherman's Wharf, this hotel is a 10-minute walk from Pier 39 and 1.1 miles from Coit Tower. Social spaces include a living room-style lobby and an outdoor courtyard. The American restaurant serves breakfast as well as all-day drinks, including Starbucks coffee. There's also a 24/7 business center with workstations and printers, and an exercise room.
